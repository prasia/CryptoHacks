Learn Crypto

Does it seem like everyone but you has been investing since they were in diapers? Let's change that.

Step 1: Select a currency to invest in.

Whether you prefer to stick to popular currencies like Bitcoin or choose ones you think to have the potential to blow up is entirely up to you. However, it is crucial to have a strategy and not put your life savings into a single currency (diversify!) Click here to find some different currencies you could invest in..

Step 2: Research the currency’s management.

While most altcoins (non-Bitcoin currencies) use blockchains (click on Watch a Video for more info), some may have a different growing method. For example, Ethereum uses decentralized applications (a.k.a “DApps”), meaning that it is controlled by many computers instead of a single entity.

Step 3: Learn the fundamentals of crypto.

Although assessing crypto is very different from your run-of-the-mill business, there are still many ways to indicate how well crypto may perform. For example, transaction value, trading volume, market capitalization, hash rate, and active addresses are great tools to help you get what you need. For more information, go to this article.

Step 4: Register for a broker.

To trade crypto, you will likely need a broker, a middleman that organizes and executes your crypto sales and purchases. There are many brokers out there, like Coinbase and Gemini, but we prefer Robinhood as there is no account minimum or fees, it costs nothing to get started, and is less prone to technical issues (in comparison to Coinbase, dated 2021).

Step 5: Purchase your desired sum.

Some currencies -like Bitcoin- are purchased using cash, while others are bought using different cryptocurrencies.

Step 6: Store your crypto.

A wallet is an online storage unit for crypto. Many brokers, like Robinhood, offer wallets, but investors could also transfer their coins into hot or cold wallets. The difference between hot and cold is that hot wallets are connected to the internet, and cold wallets are not.
